Development of a Low Cost Prosthesis

With a population of 1.2 billion as of 2014, it is estimated by the National Sample Survey Organization that there are 1.8% disabled citizens in India. This amounts to a whooping number of 960 million disabilities in India with the majority of them being locomotor disabilities (about 40%).
Amputees being 8% of these, there are approximately an astounding 30 million amputees in India.

This research works towards the development of a prosthetic hand costing below $400, which replicates natural movement of the fingers and provides to help amputees perform basic functions. A bottom up design approach is employed, incorporating only the necessary features so as to bring down the cost of the prosthesis without compromising on reliability. The design and development of this prosthetic arm is targeted towards the rural population. This would prove to be beneficial to arm amputees who cannot otherwise afford existing prosthesis to perform basic day to day activities.

Conference Paper:
Design and Development of a Low Cost Prosthetic Hand (ICACDOT2016)

 

 

 

A special application specific electromyography sensor was designed for this purpose, incorporating modular interconnected blocks which can be changed depending on circumstances. Reliable signal based on muscle movement of the patient was obtained through hardware and software filtering. A three finger, lead screw based mechanism allowed users to hold objects and perform everyday actions. The mechanism was actuated through a DC motor along with the lead screw mechanism for power transmission.
